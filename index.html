Monte Carlo Pi Simulation

In this test, we are attempting to use monte carlo simulation to estimate the value of pi. We start with a square (lenth=width=height=2), and inscribe a unit circle inside of it (r=1.0). This means the area of the circle is pi, and the area of the square is 4. Put as a ratio (called p) of area of circle compared to area of square, we have p = pi/4. So, what we want to do is estimate the ratio with monte carlo simulation, and multiply it by 4 -- thus giving an estimation of pi.

We will be using html5 canvas. See how the pretty circle is in the pretty box? Good. When you click the "Run Simulation" button below, we will run a number of iterations (that you control) and estimate pi.
